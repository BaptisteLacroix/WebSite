CyberSecurity

Cybercrime is a never-ending threat.

You may think that the only form of cybercrime you should be concerned about is the theft of your financial data. But it is not that simple. There are other dangers than those that weigh on your finances. Cybercrime is constantly evolving and creating new threats.

When you hear about crimes online, you may be tempted to stop using the Internet at all. This reaction would however be a bit radical. Instead, to protect your privacy and data, you can start by learning how to recognize cyber threats. Taking simple precautions and knowing who to contact when you see people engaging in criminal activity online are also important habits to adopt. You may want to learn how to prevent cyber threats, but here it is: it's impossible. You can however take precautions to protect yourself from it.

CyberCriminality ?

Espionage, theft of encrypted sensitive data, fraud, sabotage, malicious acts ... The reasons for cyberattacks and hacking are more and more numerous and their targets are just as numerous. States targeted from abroad as companies can be attacked in different ways, from the simple saturation of the computer network by denial of service (DDoS) to advanced spyware, including the clearing of a site pirated by hackers.

Cybercrime includes all crimes that are committed online or primarily online. Cybercriminals often commit crimes by targeting networks or computer devices. Cybercrime includes several types of malicious acts ranging from the exploitation of a security breach to the theft of personal data

But what is CyberSecurity then?

Cybersecurity is an area that brings together laws, policies, devices, concepts and mechanisms of security, applied to the digital world and used for the protection of people and computer equipment of States and organizations. Cybersecurity, a field that is still growing, is closely linked to the process of control and communication between people and machines
