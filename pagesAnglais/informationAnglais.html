1. Update your software

To protect yourself from cybercrime, the first recommendation is to update your operating system and software whenever necessary. Indeed, as recalled by the National Agency for Security and Information Systems, obsolete programs, systems, software or applications increase the risks and facilitate possible computer attacks.

2. Choose strong passwords

Protecting yourself against cybercrime involves simple reflexes, starting with effective choices of passwords. For more security and to make pirating this precious sesame more complicated, opt for unique and different passwords for each account or device. Likewise, do not choose one that is too obvious (such as "admin" or "company name + year") and use various characters: spaces, capital letters, numbers, symbols.

3. Check your social media settings

Protect your personal and confidential data on social networks. Cybercriminals can often access your personal data from a minimum of information. Therefore, the less information you share publicly, the safer you are. For example, if you publish your pet's name or reveal your mother's maiden name, you can expose the answers to two common safety questions.

4. Strengthen your security on Wi-Fi networks

To get started, it's a good idea to use a strong encryption password as well as a virtual private network when using public Wi-Fi. A VPN (short for Virtual Private Network) encrypts all outgoing traffic from your devices until it arrives at its destination. If cybercriminals successfully hack your line of communication, they will not intercept anything other than encrypted data. It's a good idea to use a VPN when connecting to a public Wi-Fi network, whether it's in a library, cafe, hotel, or airport.

5. Maintain a permanent dialogue with your children

You can teach your children good habits about using the Internet while maintaining a constructive dialogue with them. Make sure they know they can go to you if they experience online harassment, spying or bullying

6. Take steps to protect yourself from theft of personal data

Personal data theft occurs when someone obtains your personal information by fraud or by trapping you, usually for profit. How is it possible ? You can be fooled into revealing personal information on the Internet, for example, or a cybercriminal can steal your physical mail to access the account information that was sent to you. This is why it is important to protect your personal data. A VPN can also help you protect the data you send and receive online, especially when accessing the Internet over a public Wi-Fi network.



The fight against cybercrime is everyone's business. Consider it an obligation to make your contribution in the fight against cybercrime. For most of us, it comes down to applying a few simple and logical rules to keep us and our family safe.
